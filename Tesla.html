About Tesla:

"The only thing I'm certain of is that I know nothing" - Socrates Johnson-Bill & Ted's Excellent Adventure


In a world of ever-evolving uncertainty, there are few ways to mitigate such risks. The first option is not to take part in the world and end up like Ted Bundy. The second option is to invest in the index's play it safe and end up with a healthy portfolio that can weather recessions and flourish in booms. The riskier and Wall Street Bets approved method is to invest in securities. If you decided on the last option, you are now part of an elite group of gamblers. The difference is you will not be comped a meal or a room for spending enough. Still, you might get a retweet from the one and only emperor of SpaceX and Tzar of Tesla: Elon Musk.


The advent of Machine Learning (ML) has been a double-edged sword in the world of investments. On the one hand, we have predicted inevitable market fluctuations that analysts and market insiders could not see for decades. While this benefit has vast potential, there also happens to be a reliance problem. The more Machine learning got right, the more traders and simple investors are more likely to trade using their input. The issue with that is: ML gets the stock market wrong just as much as it gets it right. The perfect example would be the unicorn of the bunch: Tesla.


Tesla has become an outlier for multiple reasons. The first and most reasonable explanation is the charismatic and sometimes volatile CEO of Tesla, Elon Musk. His vision to bring an old concept into the forefront of the green movement was a stroke of vision and brilliance. However, his bad-boy antics and constant need for attention have driven many shifts in Tesla stock up and down. The second reason for the meteoric rise of Tesla has been attributed to its vision in respect to the global goals of becoming net-zero carbon emissions. Environment crusades have become a large part of the 21st century. Any company that bases its product around this idea enjoys business morality and die-hard supporters towards a unifying goal. The final and most profound impact would be the Innovator status. Electric vehicles have been around since the 1880s (1). Still, Tesla was the first company to make it not only mass-produced but sexy as well. Every other car firm in the market is trying to catch up to Tesla. At the same time, Tesla enjoys a large share of the electric car market.


To summarize, ML is an excellent tool to predict a stock's outcomes, even a meteoric one such as Tesla. We must keep in mind that even the smartest ML algorithm can be made a liar by two things: volatility in the market and Elon Musk going on The Joe Rogan Experience.
